## Section 1 — Independent Objective Assessment

*This section sets aside the family's specific situation entirely. It answers a different question: of these eight homes, which are the best real estate purchases by any reasonable measure? The criteria applied consistently across all eight homes: (1) comp position — how does the asking price compare to what similar nearby homes actually sold for? (2) location market tier — stability, liquidity, appreciation trajectory; (3) asset configuration — beds, baths, sqft, lot, unique features; (4) financial efficiency — loan remaining after recast, payoff speed, total interest, best-case monthly floor; (5) post-payoff ongoing cost — tax + HOA + insurance, which is the permanent housing floor regardless of mortgage; (6) build risk — age, systems, materials; (7) exit/resale floor — what happens if you need to sell?*

*The family's commute preference, office needs, bathroom tolerance, and debt-free timeline are not factors in this section.*

### Objective #1 — 5866 Ogilby Dr, Hudson OH

**$375,000 · 4 bed / 2.5 bath · 2,016 sqft · Built 1974**

#### The Comp Gap Is the Story

Five comparable Hudson homes sold between $425,000 and $480,000 in the last eight months. Ogilby is listed at $375,000 — $50,000 to $105,000 below every single one. Same zip code. Similar configuration. This is not a subtle discount; it is a structural gap that would attract any serious buyer regardless of their personal circumstances.

That gap translates directly: if you pay $375,000 today and sell at the average comp of $445,000 two years from now, that is $70,000 in equity gain on top of whatever principal you've paid down. No other home on this list comes close to that exit trajectory.

#### Hudson Is the Right Market

Hudson sits at the top of the greater Akron suburban tier — stable values, strong demand, smaller buyer pool volatility. It outperforms Stow, Kent, Aurora, and Ravenna in appreciation rate and liquidity across market cycles. Buying in Hudson at a meaningful discount is the best combination of market quality and entry price available in this search.

#### The Home Itself Has No Structural Weaknesses

4 bed / 2.5 bath / 2,016 sqft is the strongest configuration reviewed. Full basement provides storm shelter, storage, and expansion potential. 0.46-acre lot. Wood-burning fireplace, deck, patio. Aluminum siding is durable and low maintenance. No crushing tax, no missing basement, no one-bathroom constraint.

The 1974 build requires a thorough inspection — roof, HVAC, electrical panel — but is not unusual for the market and carries moderate (not high) risk.

#### Post-Payoff Ongoing Cost: $536/mo

Tax ($436) + insurance ($100). Not the lowest on the list, but entirely reasonable for Hudson. There is no HOA.

#### What This Ranking Does Not Claim

Paying off a $170,000 loan requires five years of $30K December bonuses landing intact. That is execution risk, not property risk. The home itself is the strongest objective purchase on this list.

## Home #1 to Visit: Charring Cross Dr, Stow

### The Financial Case Is Unmatched

After putting $25K down and recasting with $180K, the remaining loan balance is **$14,900**. The first December bonus — $30,000 — is nearly double that amount. This home is paid off in approximately one year from closing. Total interest paid over the life of the loan: **~$900**. Not per month. Total.

That is the 5-year debt-free goal achieved in year one, leaving four full years of financial runway before the AI threat window even closes.

At payoff, monthly housing cost drops to **$433/mo** (tax + insurance only). That number is sustainable on a significantly reduced salary, on a part-time income, or as a bridge on savings. It is genuine financial security.

### Does It Work for the Family?

**Space:** 1,591 sqft. They currently live well in 1,500 sqft. This is more house than they use every day right now.

**Bedrooms:** 3 bedrooms. The math: parents in one room, 6-year-old in one room, 7-month-old in the parents' room for now. That leaves one room as a home office — one office, not two. This is a real constraint. For the first year or two, one parent has a dedicated office and the other uses a shared space or the living room. It's not ideal but it's not unique either — millions of people work from home without a dedicated office. More critically, once the mortgage is gone at the end of year one, there's cash to add on, convert space, or simply not care because the financial pressure is gone.

**Bathrooms:** 1 bathroom. The family has evaluated this themselves and concluded it works for ~3 years. The 7-month-old adds no bathroom competition. The 6-year-old and two adults on a morning schedule is manageable. This is a real inconvenience, not a dealbreaker, given the timeline.

**Basement:** None. This is the one genuine structural gap versus what they have now. No storm shelter, no extra storage, no overflow space. Ohio weather makes the no-basement situation worth noting.

**Location:** Stow, 14 min to school. Stow is a desirable suburb — familiar community, access to Akron amenities, no HOA.

**Comps:** Six comparable Stow sales ranged $179,500–$270,000. Charring Cross is the largest home in that comp set at the second-lowest price. Four of the six smaller comparable homes sold for more. The market supports the price.

**Condition:** Personal rating 8/10 condition, 9/10 potential. The family has already looked at this one and liked what they saw.

**Just listed:** No DOM history, no prior seller behavior to read. Come in with a fair offer — the comps don't support aggressive lowballing, but the asking price is already at the low end of the comp range.

### The Honest Tradeoff

One bathroom and one home office (not two) for a period measured in months, in exchange for being mortgage-free before the next year is out. The 1962 build means an inspection is non-negotiable — verify the mechanicals, roof, and electrical panel condition before any offer.

### What to Look At During the Showing

- Inspect the bathroom carefully — condition, size, whether a second bath could ever be added
- Look at the layout for a second office solution (finished basement potential? No. But could a room be subdivided? Could one parent work from the living room or kitchen area comfortably?)
- Age of roof, HVAC, water heater
- No basement — ask about drainage, grading, and where the family would shelter in a storm
- Garage condition and whether it's insulated/heated (usable workspace in Ohio winters)
